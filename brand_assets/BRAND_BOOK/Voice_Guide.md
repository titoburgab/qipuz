## Voice Character

QIPUZ communicates with five qualities held in tension:

**Confident** — We know what we are doing and we say so directly. We do not hedge unnecessarily or qualify claims that do not require qualification.

**Intelligent** — We engage with complexity rather than simplifying it away. We write for people who are capable of understanding nuance.

**Precise** — We use the right word. We do not reach for synonyms when the accurate term serves better. We do not pad sentences that are complete at a shorter length.

**Human** — We build for people. Our writing acknowledges that the people reading it have context, judgment, and their own intelligence. We do not condescend.

**Clear** — The test for every sentence: would a capable professional with no prior knowledge of QIPUZ understand this on a single reading? If not, rewrite it.

## Sentence Construction

### Use active voice
The subject acts. The product works. QIPUZ builds. Passive constructions dilute accountability and obscure meaning.

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| "Complexity is managed by the system." | "The system manages complexity." |
| "Results are delivered to clients." | "We deliver measurable results." |
| "This feature was designed to..." | "This feature reduces..." |

### Make claims specific
Vague claims erode trust. Specific claims build it.

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| "We improve efficiency." | "We automate the three most time-consuming steps in your approval workflow." |
| "Our AI is powerful." | "Our agents process 10,000 documents per hour with 94% classification accuracy." |
| "We deliver results." | "Clients typically reduce manual processing time by 60–80%." |

### Match sentence length to content
Short sentences for declarative claims. Longer sentences for explanation and nuance. Vary the rhythm. A sequence of identically-structured sentences reads as if it was generated, not written.

### End strongly
The last word of a sentence carries disproportionate weight. Avoid ending on prepositions, qualifiers, or weak nouns.

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| "This is something we care deeply about." | "We care deeply about this." → better: "This matters." |
| "Results that are measurable and lasting are what we provide." | "We deliver measurable, lasting results." |

## Vocabulary

### Words we use
- engineer, build, design, develop (not "create solutions")
- system, platform, architecture, infrastructure (not "ecosystem")
- precise, reliable, scalable, explainable (our engineering adjectives)
- augment, amplify, accelerate (AI's role)
- client (not customer)
- engagement (not project, for client relationships)
- ship (for releasing work, internally)

### Words we avoid
| Avoid | Because |
|---|---|
| "revolutionary" | Claimed by everything; means nothing |
| "cutting-edge" | Cliché; signals marketing, not engineering |
| "next-generation" | Cliché |
| "seamless" | Claimed by everything |
| "leverage" (as verb) | Corporate jargon |
| "synergy" | Never |
| "solution" (generic) | Vague; say what the solution does |
| "ecosystem" | Overused in tech marketing |
| "disruptive" | Dead word |
| "game-changing" | Dead word |
| "empower" (used loosely) | Meaningful only when specific |

## Tone by Context

The QIPUZ character is consistent. The register adjusts by context.

### Website (Homepage, Services)
Register: confident, compelling, accessible.
Purpose: communicate what we do and why it matters.
Sentence length: short to medium. Breathing room between ideas.

*Example:*
> "Most organizations don't have an AI problem. They have a complexity problem. AI is how we solve it."

### Technical Documentation
Register: precise, instructional, complete.
Purpose: communicate exactly what something does and how to use it.
No marketing language. Every claim must be demonstrable.

*Example:*
> "The agent processes incoming requests in parallel across a maximum of 12 concurrent threads. Request queuing activates when the active thread count reaches the defined threshold."

### Proposals
Register: authoritative, specific, outcome-focused.
Purpose: demonstrate that we understand the client's problem and have a credible path to solving it.
Avoid generic claims. Every paragraph should contain something that is specific to this client.

### Email and Direct Communication
Register: direct, professional, human.
Purpose: advance a conversation or relationship.
Be brief. Respect the reader's time. No filler paragraphs.

### Social and Editorial
Register: confident, occasionally opinionated.
Purpose: demonstrate thought leadership and attract the right attention.
A stronger point of view is acceptable here than in client-facing materials.

## Punctuation and Formatting

- Use an em dash — like this — for parenthetical emphasis. Not a hyphen.
- The Oxford comma is required: "clarity, precision, and trust."
- Periods after every sentence, including in lists where items are complete sentences.
- No exclamation marks in brand copy. Confidence does not require punctuation.
- Headline case for H1 and H2 in web contexts; sentence case for H3 and below.
- Numbers under ten are spelled out in editorial contexts; numerals in all technical and data contexts.

## What Good QIPUZ Writing Sounds Like

**On the homepage:**
> "QIPUZ engineers intelligent systems, AI agents, and workflow automations. We help organizations stop managing complexity and start operating through it."

**In a case study:**
> "The client's onboarding process involved 14 manual steps across four departments. We automated 11 of them, reduced average onboarding time from 12 days to 3, and eliminated the category of errors that had caused the most escalations."

**In documentation:**
> "The routing agent evaluates incoming requests against a priority matrix defined in the configuration file. Requests that match no defined rule are escalated to the human review queue, not dropped."

**In a proposal:**
> "The core problem is not a lack of data. It is that the relevant data is spread across six systems with no unified access layer. We will build that layer."

## What Poor QIPUZ Writing Sounds Like

*Too vague:*
> "We leverage AI to deliver synergistic solutions that empower your team to reach its full potential."

*Too cold:*
> "System input is processed via NLP pipeline. Output vectors are ranked by relevance score prior to presentation layer rendering."

*Too tentative:*
> "We might be able to help with some of your workflow challenges if that would potentially be of interest."

*Too promotional:*
> "Our revolutionary, cutting-edge AI platform will transform your organization's performance!"
