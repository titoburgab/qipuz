## The Market Landscape

The AI services market has three populated tiers:

**Tier 1: AI Tool Vendors**
Companies that sell AI-powered software products: subscriptions, SaaS platforms, pre-built agents. The market is saturated. Differentiation is largely on pricing and feature surface.

**Tier 2: Large Consulting Firms**
Accenture, Deloitte, McKinsey AI practices. They have enterprise relationships and deep vertical expertise. Their AI work is often slow, expensive, and relies on the same off-the-shelf tools available to everyone.

**Tier 3: AI Development Agencies**
Small to mid-size agencies that implement AI tools for clients. Variable quality. Often prototype-to-production gap. Limited governance and engineering depth.

**QIPUZ's position:** Between Tier 2 and Tier 3 — with the engineering rigor that most agencies lack, at the speed and agility that large consulting firms cannot deliver.

## Who We Compete Against

**In practice, QIPUZ competes against:**
- The client's internal team ("we can build this ourselves")
- Mid-size AI development agencies
- Freelance AI engineers / small teams
- Boutique AI consultancies

**We do not typically compete against:**
- Enterprise consulting firms (different client size and budget)
- AI platform vendors (different category)

## The Objection We Must Answer

*"Why not just hire an AI engineer or use a cheaper agency?"*

> "You can. The question is whether you want to manage the engineering, governance, and long-term maintenance of an AI system in addition to using it. QIPUZ delivers a complete system — designed, documented, tested, governed, and ready to hand off to your team. The alternative is not cheaper when you account for the full cost of ownership."

## The Story We Tell

The QIPUZ story is not about AI.

It is about the gap between what AI promises and what organizations actually experience when they deploy it.

We tell the story of organizations that tried AI tools and got noise instead of intelligence. Of systems that worked in demos and failed in production. Of automation that saved time in one place and created problems in three others.

Then we explain what it looks like when AI is engineered properly.

That is the QIPUZ story.
